There are 21 user stories (worth 55 story points, or equivalent to 53% of overall story points) that we planned to complete in sprint 2. All of these are the remaining priority 1 effort (highest priority) that were not part of Sprint 1. However, we were only able to complete 64% of the stories we planned to do during Sprint 2 (not being able to complete 7 stories). Overall, we have completed 75% of the story points.

Because we had an amazing team (there are 2 members of the team who are super-coders/ CSers, as we’ve mentioned last week), we were able to complete a lot of progress during sprint 2. So much so, that we were able to complete 10 story points over what we accomplished in the previous sprint (35 in sprint 2 vs. 24 in sprint 1). Many of the completed stories relate to the basic functionalities of the website, such as: creating new events, event title, event location and date/time, and ability to see those information on the events page. One of the best progress we accomplished is developing an enhanced view of the website, where the different events are presented as tiles with vibrant images.

Challenges Faced:

 

We were not able to do 36% of the work that we were planning to complete in sprint 2. This is due to the increased level of work that we didn’t expect in creating an event. The data validation for the form was tougher than we predicted, and troubleshooting these issues took the majority of our time during the sprint. Due to this, we were not able to complete several RSVP and donation related stories.
